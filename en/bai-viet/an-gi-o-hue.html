Must-Try Hue Dishes & Cozy Local Eateries

Published: June 2026 Category: Local Cuisine Reading Time: 6-Min Read

Hue culinary style is not just delicious—it is a sophisticated heritage. Royal ancestors refined meals into delicate, small portions rich in spicy, sweet, and comforting dynamics. Below is a curated food list from the Fairy’s House hosts, highlighting local diners beloved by residents.

1. Bun Bo Hue - A Spicy, Robust Noodle Soup

This world-renowned soup features a rich, lemongrass-filled broth simmered for hours with beef bones, shrimp paste, and mild chili oil. Tender sliced beef, pork knuckles, and cubes of congealed blood pair with thick rice noodles and fresh green herbs like banana blossoms for a perfect meal.

📍 Recommended Spots, Addresses & Hours:

  • Bun Bo Mu Roi:
    📍 Address: 40 Nguyen Chi Dieu Street, Thuan Thanh Ward, Hue.
    ⏰ Expected hours: 06:30 AM – 11:00 AM & 04:00 PM – 09:00 PM.
  • Bun Bo Me Keo:
    📍 Address: 20 Bach Dang Street, Phu Cat Ward, Hue.
    ⏰ Expected hours: 06:00 AM – 10:00 AM (Arrive before 08:30 AM as it sells out quickly).

2. The Iconic Cake Trio: Banh Beo, Banh Nam & Banh Bot Loc

Hue specialty cakes are light, aromatic bites crafted from rice or tapioca flour, then steamed in banana leaves:

  • Banh Beo: Tiny, steamed rice cakes served in clay saucers, topped with savory minced shrimp, crispy fried shallots, and sweet fish sauce.
  • Banh Nam: Flat, delicate rice batter packages filled with a savory mixture of minced pork and shrimp.
  • Banh Bot Loc: Translucent, chewy tapioca dumplings stuffed with whole caramelized river shrimp and pork belly, served with a sweet and spicy dipping sauce.

📍 Recommended Spots, Addresses & Hours:

  • Quan Ba Do:
    📍 Address: 8 Nguyen Binh Khiem Street, Phu Cat Ward, Hue.
    ⏰ Expected hours: 08:00 AM – 09:30 PM.
  • Banh Beo Me Chieu:
    📍 Address: Kiet 64 Nguyen Hue Street, Vinh Ninh Ward, Hue (Near An Cuu).
    ⏰ Expected hours: 01:30 PM – 07:30 PM (Cakes freshly steamed to order).

3. Com Hen & Bun Hen - Savory, Spicy Baby Clam Dishes

Com Hen (Baby Clam Rice) is a humble masterpiece. It pairs cooled rice with tiny sweet baby clams harvested from the Huong River, crispy pork rinds, fried shallots, roasted peanuts, sesame seeds, and a mix of fresh herbs. It is served with a bowl of warm, ginger-infused clam broth and savory fermented shrimp paste (Mắm Ruốc).

📍 Recommended Spots, Addresses & Hours:

  • Com Hen Hoa Dong:
    📍 Address: Kiet 64 Ung Binh Street, Vy Da Ward, Hue.
    ⏰ Expected hours: 07:00 AM – 10:00 PM.
  • Com Hen Me Nho:
    📍 Address: Kiet 1 Han Mac Tu Street, Vy Da Ward, Hue.
    ⏰ Expected hours: 06:00 AM – 01:00 PM (Perfect for early breakfast or lunch).

4. Che Hem - Sweet Soups tucked away in Quiet Alleys

Hue features more than 36 kinds of colorful sweet dessert soups (Chè), traditionally served in small glasses. "Chè Hẻm" (Alley Sweet Soup) offers everything from rich lotus seed pudding to the unique Che Bot Loc Heo Quay—sweet, chewy tapioca balls filled with savory roasted pork belly.

📍 Recommended Spots, Addresses & Hours:

  • Che Hem Hung Vuong:
    📍 Address: Kiet 1 Hung Vuong Street, Phu Hoi Ward, Hue.
    ⏰ Expected hours: 02:00 PM – 10:00 PM.
